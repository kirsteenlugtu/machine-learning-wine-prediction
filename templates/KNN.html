K-Nearest Neighbors

K-Nearest Neighbors (KNN) classifies values based on proximity, calculating the distance between points and assuming similar values are closer together. To find the best vale of K for the model, we tested a range of values and selected the lowest point where the score starts to stabalize.

The red wine test accuracy score reached .583 at its highest. White wine reached .548 accuracy before stabilizing. The algorithm is slowed down by the multiple qualifiers in the data set, leading to lower accuracy scores


Red Wine KNN Plot

The above graph shows the K-Nearest Neighbors plot for the red wine data. We can see the plot start to even out around k=31 with a training score of .601 and testing score of .583. This model accurately predicts the quality of red wine about 58% of the time, making this an unreliable model.

White Wine KNN Plot

The above graph shows the K-Nearest Neighbors plot for the whitewine data. We can see the plot start to even out around k=46 with a training score of .581 and testing score of .548. This model accurately predicts the quality of red wine about 55% of the time, making this an unreliable model.
